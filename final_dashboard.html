Technology Usage & Mental Well-Being Dashboard

Descriptive trend analysis of technology use and well-being outcomes (cross-sectional survey data)

Overview

This dashboard explores descriptive patterns in technology usage and mental well-being using a structured, cross-sectional survey dataset. The visualizations focus on trends and distributions across respondents to understand how screen time, gaming, and social media use relate to sleep, physical activity, stress, and self-reported mental health. Findings are presented as associations rather than cause-and-effect relationships.

Key Findings

As a human researcher, the relationship between technology usage, sleep, stress, and mental health shows consistent patterns across the visualizations. Median sleep duration remains relatively stable across lower to moderate screen time levels. However, at higher screen time levels, median sleep duration begins to decline, with typical sleep falling closer to or below commonly recommended guidelines. This pattern suggests that heavier screen exposure is associated with shorter sleep duration for many respondents.

The distribution-based visualizations further show how behaviors cluster across the population. In the comparison between gaming and physical activity levels, higher gaming durations are more commonly observed alongside lower levels of physical activity. Similarly, the screen time by gender heatmap shows that moderate and high screen time categories contain the majority of respondents across all gender groups, indicating that higher screen use is widespread rather than concentrated within a specific demographic.

When examining mental health status, average daily technology usage appears slightly higher among respondents who reported poorer mental health outcomes. Although the difference is modest, the pattern suggests an association between increased technology use and less favorable mental health status. A related trend shows that higher technology and social media usage levels correspond with small reductions in average sleep duration.

Overall, the visualizations reveal gradual and consistent patterns within the data. Higher levels of technology and social media use tend to align with less favorable sleep and well-being outcomes. Because the dataset is cross-sectional, these patterns should be interpreted as associations rather than cause-and-effect relationships.

Trend Analysis Process

Trend analysis was conducted using descriptive visualizations suited for cross-sectional survey data. Continuous technology usage variables were summarized using binned medians and grouped averages to reduce noise and highlight typical respondent behavior. Categorical mental health indicators were examined using bar charts and count-based heatmaps to preserve their qualitative nature.

Limitations

Several limitations should be considered when interpreting this dashboard. First, the data are cross-sectional and represent respondents at a single point in time, which prevents conclusions about causality or the direction of relationships. Second, all measures are self-reported and may be influenced by recall bias or subjective interpretation. Third, the analysis does not account for potential confounding factors such as age, occupation, academic workload, or lifestyle differences. Finally, continuous variables were grouped into ranges and summarized at the group level for dashboard clarity, which may mask some individual-level variation.
